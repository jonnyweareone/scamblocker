## Why HMRC Scam Calls Work So Well

HMRC scams are the most successful phone fraud in Britain for three reasons:

### 1. **The Authority Effect**
When someone says "HMRC," your brain shifts into compliance mode. Especially if you're elderly and grew up respecting government institutions. Scammers know this.

### 2. **The Fear Trigger**  
"Arrest warrant" + "police coming" + "urgent" = panic. In panic, we don't think clearly. We just want the threat to stop.

### 3. **The Isolation Trap**
They tell you: "Don't hang up. Don't tell anyone. This is a criminal investigation."

So you don't call your daughter. You don't check online. You just pay.

## How to Spot a Fake HMRC Call

**HMRC will NEVER:**
- Threaten you with immediate arrest
- Demand payment over the phone
- Ask for payment in gift cards, Bitcoin, or bank transfer
- Tell you not to hang up or speak to family
- Use threatening language or create panic
- Ask for your full bank details or PIN

**HMRC will ALWAYS:**
- Send letters first (multiple letters)
- Give you time to respond (weeks, not hours)
- Allow you to verify through official channels
- Let you get independent advice
