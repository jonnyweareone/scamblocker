**Your mum presses 1.**

**It's not.**

## Why Bank Scam Calls Are So Effective

### 1. They Have Your Details Already
- They know your name
- They know your bank
- They know your phone number
- Sometimes they know your address

**Where from?** Data breaches, previous scams, bought "sucker lists."

**Why it works:** When they say your name and bank, your brain thinks "They must be real."

### 2. They Create Instant Panic
"Your account has been compromised."  
"Someone is trying to withdraw £2,000."  
"We need to act NOW to protect your money."

**Panic short-circuits rational thinking.**

### 3. They Sound Exactly Like Your Bank
- Professional tone
- Banking jargon
- Security questions
- "Fraud prevention team"
- On-hold music (sometimes)

**You literally cannot tell the difference.**

## The Three Types of Bank Scam Calls

### Type 1: Fake Fraud Alert

**The Hook:** "We've detected suspicious activity on your account."

**The Scam:** They ask you to "verify" transactions (that never happened), then ask you to move money to a "safe account" to protect it.

**The Steal:** The "safe account" is theirs. Money gone instantly.

---

### Type 2: Fake Security Update

**The Hook:** "We're updating our security systems. We need to verify your details."

**The Scam:** They ask for card details, PIN, online banking password "to confirm it's really you."

**The Steal:** They now have full access to your accounts.

---

### Type 3: Fake Refund Scam

**The Hook:** "You're owed a refund of £347 from an incorrect charge."

**The Scam:** "To process the refund, we need to verify your account details."

**The Steal:** No refund exists. They steal info and empty your account.

---

## What Your Real Bank Will NEVER Do

**Your bank will NEVER:**
- Call and ask for your PIN
- Ask for your full card number
- Ask for online banking password
- Tell you to move money to a "safe account"
- Ask you to buy gift cards or Bitcoin
- Tell you not to tell anyone
- Threaten to close your account
- Create urgency with "act now" pressure
- Ask you to hand over your card to a courier

**Your bank WILL:**
- Send letters and emails first
- Let you call them back on the number on your card
- Allow time to think
- Encourage you to check with family

**Opening:**

**Why it works:** They sound helpful. They know some of your details.

**Why it works:** Of course you didn't! These are fake. But now you're panicked.

**The Hook:**
"We need to secure your account immediately. I'm going to transfer you to our secure line to verify some details. Stay on the line - if you hang up, we can't protect your account."

**Why it works:** You're scared. You want protection. You trust them.

**The Steal:**
"To secure your account, I need to verify your card details. Can you read me the long number on the front of your card, expiry date, and the three digits on the back?"

**Game over.** They now have everything they need.

## Why "Just Hang Up" Doesn't Work

Everyone says: "If it's your bank, hang up and call them back using the number on your card."

**Great advice. But here's why it fails:**

1. **The panic is too strong** - "But they said it's urgent!"
2. **They keep the line open** - You hang up, dial your bank, but you're still connected to the scammer (they never disconnected)
3. **They know you'll try this** - "Of course you can call us back! The number is..." (gives you their number)
4. **Elderly brains process slower** - By the time rational thought kicks in, they've already given details

**The solution isn't advice. It's prevention.**

## Why Elderly People Are Targeted

Scammers specifically call people over 65 because:

1. **More trusting of authority** (grew up respecting banks)
2. **Less tech-savvy** (don't verify online)
3. **More money in accounts** (lifetime savings)
4. **Home during the day** (answer landlines)
5. **Less likely to report** (shame/embarrassment)
6. **Slower to realize** (gives scammers time)
