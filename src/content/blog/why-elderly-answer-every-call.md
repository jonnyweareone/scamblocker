## What You Don't Understand About Being Old

When you're 30, 40, 50 - you have a phone full of contacts. You recognize most numbers. Unknown calls are usually spam.

**When you're 75:**
- The doctor's surgery calls from different numbers each time
- The pharmacy uses a mobile
- Your grandchild might be calling from a friend's phone
- The hospital doesn't always come up as "Hospital"
- Your daughter might be calling from work (different number)

**Every unknown number could be important.**

And the one time she doesn't answer? That might be the call about Dad's test results. Or you, calling from a new number because yours broke.

**She can't take that risk.**

---

## The Scammer's Greatest Weapon: Her Love For You

Scammers know this. They use it.

**Their opening lines:**
- "It's about your grandson"
- "There's been an accident"  
- "Your daughter needs help"
- "Family emergency"

**Her brain goes:** Unknown number → Could be about my child → MUST ANSWER

By the time she realizes it's a scam, she's already engaged. Already emotional. Already vulnerable.

## Why "Just Let It Ring" Is Impossible Advice

**For you:** Unknown call = probably spam = ignore it  
**For her:** Unknown call = might be important = must answer

You've got WhatsApp, text, email, social media. Twenty ways to reach people.

**She's got the landline.**

That's it. That's her connection to the world.

When it rings, she answers. Because to her, it's not an "unknown number."

**It's hope.**

## The Phone Anxiety Loop

Here's what happens in her brain:

**Ring #1:** Maybe it's important → Answer → Scam call → Feel stupid  
**Ring #2:** Don't want to be scammed → Don't answer → Voicemail is from doctor → Panic  
**Ring #3:** Can't miss important calls → Answer → Scam call → Feel stupid  
**Ring #4:** Now anxious every time the phone rings

Eventually, she's either:
- Answering everything (gets scammed)
- Answering nothing (misses important calls)

**Both options are terrible.**

## Why Voicemail Doesn't Work for Elderly People

"Let scammers leave voicemail, then you'll know it's not real!"

**Except:**
- Many scammers DO leave voicemail
- She doesn't always check voicemail
- Scam voicemails sound real ("Call back urgently")
- Real calls sometimes don't leave messages
- She has to listen to threatening scam messages

**Plus:** Scammers often say "Don't hang up, don't tell anyone, call this number back immediately."

So she calls them back. And now she's initiated contact (looks more legitimate).
